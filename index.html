Historical preview signal Dot Foods Manchester distribution center opening
Account profile
Dot Foods opened a Manchester, Tennessee distribution center in 2024. The historical signal shows a food redistribution node with multi-temperature freight relevance and regional operating impact.
Market
Foodservice redistribution, multi-temperature warehouse flow, inbound supplier movement, and outbound distributor or foodservice replenishment.
Region
Manchester, Tennessee with Southeast coverage relevance across Tennessee, Alabama, Mississippi, Kentucky, and Georgia.
Evidence source
Dot Foods newsroom, July 30, 2024: Manchester distribution center opening .
Trigger read
New distribution center opening, warehouse role activity, Class A CDL driver hiring, and stated regional foodservice coverage.
Freight read
Possible ramp-period pressure across replenishment flow, inbound supplier freight, temperature-controlled capacity, dry van balance, and backup coverage.
Contact path
Operations leadership, transportation leadership, facility-level distribution management, and recruiting/driver operations route.
Access path
Public company contact routes, location-level phone path, LinkedIn role targeting, and enrichment status.
Timing window
Historical ramp window; new distribution nodes often expose lane imbalance, pickup timing friction, and overflow capacity gaps.
Outreach position
Lead with the operating change and offer a short ramp-period coverage review across lanes, vendor pickups, delivery windows, and backup capacity.
Source notes
Primary source quality, corroboration status, observed-language notes, scoring rationale, and compliance-safe phrasing.
